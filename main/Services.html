Tests

Urine Test

Urine tests provide insights into metabolic and kidney functions, helping detect infections or other health issues early.

Ultrasound

Ultrasounds use sound waves to create images, helping assess internal organs, tissues, and blood flow for comprehensive care.

X-ray

X-rays allow visualization of bones and other structures, assisting in diagnosing fractures, infections, and other conditions.

CT Scan

CT scans use X-rays to create detailed images, often aiding in detecting abnormalities and planning medical procedures.

ECG

An ECG records the heart's electrical signals to help detect heart conditions, such as arrhythmias, at an early stage.

Blood Tests

Routine Hematology

Measures components in blood, including white cells and platelets, to assess overall health and detect blood disorders.

Coagulation

Evaluates blood clotting ability, essential for diagnosing clotting disorders and planning surgeries safely.

Routine Chemistry

Tests basic metabolic functions, including electrolyte balance, glucose levels, and kidney function indicators.

Renal Function

Analyzes kidney health by measuring blood markers, aiding in diagnosing renal conditions and monitoring treatments.

Liver Function

Assesses liver enzymes and proteins to evaluate liver health and detect conditions such as hepatitis or cirrhosis.

Pancreas Function

Monitors pancreatic enzyme levels, which are crucial in diagnosing conditions like pancreatitis or diabetes.

Endocrinology

Examines hormone levels to detect imbalances affecting metabolism, thyroid function, and overall endocrine health.

Tumor Markers

Detects markers associated with various cancers, enabling early diagnosis and monitoring of treatment responses.
